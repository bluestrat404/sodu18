South Durham is a church in Durham, North Carolina, near Chapel Hill. We are a vibrant family of Christians who love God, the Bible, and our neighbors. We welcome you to join us for worship and children's programs any Sunday.

Planning your visit

What to Expect

When you arrive, look for South Durham Church signs to guide you into the parking lot. Friendly faces will be waiting to greet you (look for the Welcome sign), hand you a bulletin, and guide you to the childcare check-in station or the sanctuary. We provide childcare for children under the age of five during our worship service.

When you enter the sanctuary, we'll make sure you find a seat. The worship service will include contemporary and traditional songs, a sermon, and Communion. An offering is collected, but guests are not expected to give. The bulletin you receive will walk you through each part of the service so you won't feel lost.

Ministries

"The Son of Man came not to be served but to serve, and to give his life as a ransom for many" — Mark 10:45. Like our Savior, we want to lay down our lives to serve others. These ministries are some of the ways we do that together as a church family.

Early Childhood and Elementary

SoDu Kids

During our worship service, children under the age of six are cared for in a fun, engaging, and secure environment where they learn about the God who loves them. Our great volunteers teach age-appropriate Bible lessons, sing songs, and play with the kids. Childcare is available for babies, and children six-years-old and older are invited to worship with the adults. Check-in for SoDu Kids is easy to find and opens 15 minutes before the service begins.

Life Together

Community Groups

Throughout the week, singles and families meet together in homes to encourage each other, study the Bible, and pray together. These groups give us the chance to go deeper in our lives together and in our walk with Christ.

Of course, you don't have to be a Christian to come — we welcome you whether you've got a lot of questions or you just want to see what the Bible and Christianity are all about. Most groups provide teaching for children through Kids in Community, too.

Middle and High Schoolers

SoDu Youth

Youth ministry at SoDu exists to come alongside parents and caregivers in the discipleship of children as they grow into adulthood. Our aim is to make spiritually mature disciples who are equipped to do the work of ministry, preparing youth for the independence of the college years and beyond. Our excellent youth leaders engage the youth in Bible study, fun activities, prayer, and discipleship relationships.

SoDu Youth meets weekly on Wednesday nights from 6:30–8:00 p.m. at a member's home in south Durham.
